Manifesto & Call for Articles
Truth is more interesting than polish.

Writing clarifies thinking. Sharing honestly builds understanding. When you articulate what drives your work, you discover what you truly care about — and so do your readers.

Antitesi is looking for short, personal pieces that pull back the curtain on work behind a thesis, a research project, or a university obsession. Not a diary. Not an abstract in disguise.

Think of it as backstage notes: the detours, discoveries, people, ideas, moments that made you care about something absurdly specific. The part that never makes it into the final dissertation.

Your voice can be sharp, dry, playful, serious — or all of them at once. The only real condition is sincerity: candor is more compelling than anything.

Show us the heart of your thesis. You have 1,000 words — what should your readers know?

Share your niche in a fresh way: a metaphor, a sketch, a mini-game, a diagram. Something that lets readers follow the logic without needing to be specialists.

Check out a few sample pieces on our website for the tone we love: clear, personal, and rooted in the work itself.

Submission Guidelines

Step 1: Write a brief pitch detailing your idea.
Step 2: If accepted, draft an article under 1,000 words.
Multimedia: Images, playlists, film references, clips, sketches — anything that accompanied your work.
Tone: Personal, clear, intellectually alive.
