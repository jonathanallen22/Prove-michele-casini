Manifesto

Truth is more interesting than polish.

Writing clarifies thinking. Sharing honestly builds understanding. When you articulate what drives your work, you discover what you truly care about — and so do your readers.

Call for Articles

Antitesi is looking for short, personal pieces that pull back the curtain on a thesis, a research project, or a university obsession.

Think of it as backstage notes: the detours, discoveries, and moments that made you care about something absurdly specific.

Your voice can be sharp, dry, playful, serious — or all of them at once. The only condition is sincerity.

Show us the heart of your thesis. You have 2,000 words — what should your readers know?

Share your niche in a fresh way: a metaphor, a sketch, a mini-game, a diagram. Something that lets readers follow the logic without being specialists.

Submission Guidelines

Step 1: Write a brief pitch detailing your idea.
Step 2: If accepted, draft an article under 2,000 words.
Multimedia: Images, playlists, film references, clips, sketches — anything that accompanied your work.
Tone: Personal, clear, intellectually alive.

Vision

Emerging from within the university, without remaining confined to it.

Antitesi begins where knowledge is produced: across students, researchers, and emerging practitioners. It redefines how that knowledge circulates by shifting the focus from results to processes. Academic work is no longer a finished product to be evaluated, but something in motion — open to encounter, extension, and transformation.

Context

Knowledge exists. But it rarely circulates beyond its origin.

Universities generate an increasing volume of highly specialised knowledge, much of which remains locked within disciplines and institutional structures. Dominant media environments favour speed and simplification, often flattening complexity.

On the Platform

A space for ongoing processes, not finished outcomes.

Antitesi hosts articles, visual work, and hybrid formats that reflect work in development. A research project can appear alongside its process. An artwork can expose its internal logic. A text can open into a wider field of inquiry. Each contribution connects to others, forming a network where people, stories, and processes intersect and evolve.

Content & Voices

Diversity as a condition of production, not a background detail.

Contributions come from different parts of the world and from varied socio-economic backgrounds — from highly mobile, international contexts to more constrained or unstable conditions. This diversity is not framed as background. It is part of how knowledge is produced.
